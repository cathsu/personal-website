Naval Postgraduate School

Space Systems Small Satellite Laboratory Intern (June 2019-August 2019)

I was tasked with building a Python simulator for the small satellite NPSAT-1. Previously, ground system checks were conducted with a replica of the main computer on NPSAT-1. This replica was made in-house and would take about one month to rebuild if broken. My goal was to develop software that would provide a more affordable option for validating ground systems. To rectify this problem, I built a simulator that would provide meaningful representation of the data that would have been sent by the actual spacecraft.

This project required me to understand the separate components of a spacecraft command and how these commands were encoded at the byte level. My software had to ensure only legitimate commands were accepted and differentiate between valid and invalid command parameters. As the commands were stored in an Excel spreadsheet, I generated a script to clean the data and store it in a Pandas dataframe, from which I could easily retrieve the information.

My software ran successfully on the ground station that was being used to monitor NPSAT-1. I wrote extensive documentation on how to set up the simulator test suite at the ground station. In addition, I was able to analyze the ground station test suite to broaden verification of simulator correctness. Ultimately, my software was used to record sample data for an NPSAT-1 detector.

Light & Motion

Software Engineer Co-op (March 2019- June 2019)

My objective during this co-op was to automate the data processing of routine, experimental data sets. I used Pandas and Numpy to clean and filter the dataset. I then produced an executable program that would take in a CSV file, extract the necessary data, convert it into multiple formats, and produce visualization of the final result in Matplotlib.

I was able to condense a 30 minute manual cleanup into a 5 minute task by reducing the data processing time by 80% and saving the company $1500 in operating costs. Furthermore, I maintained close contact with members of the engineering department to ensure the program was meeting design specifications.

Monterey Peninsula College

STEM Core Supplemental Instruction Leader (July 2018 - June 2019)

During the 2018-2019 academic year, I served as a Supplemental Instruction (SI) Leader for STEM Core, a one year program at Monterey Peninsula College geared towards students interested in pursuing a computer science major.

As an SI leader, I not only tutored and went over homework problems that students were having trouble with, but I also equipped students with the learning tools they needed to complete their assignments and understand the concepts they were learning in class. The idea behind SI sessions is that giving students a low stress environment to further engage in course content would help them become more successful learners.

The Python and Java sessions, which I developed, led, and facilitated, were held twice a week. In addition, I led one-on-one tutoring sessions to provide further academic support to underprepared students and collaborated with faculty on plans to improve student progress. I also assisted in the planning and running of algebra and precalculus supplemental instruction sessions. Moreover I fostered professional development by conducting an internship workshop, editing applications, and conducting mock interviews.
